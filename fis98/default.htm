Virtual Conference on the Foundations of Information Science

January - December, 1998. Internet

Organization: Pedro C. Marijuán

DISCUSSION GUIDELINES

The FIS list is an open forum. Temporary visitors are admitted both into our coming Virtual Conference and free discussions. The discussion rules to abide by, particularly during the Virtual Conference, are as follows:

Before sending long texts, or "PRESENTATIONS" (in general, up to 4 pages), regular participants and temporary visitors are kindly requested to send them in advance to the moderators (see their addresses below) in order to establish an adequate rythm of presentations. Or at least, if authors cannot send their texts in advance, permission to put them into the FIS list on a particular date has to be asked in advance to the moderators.

Short texts, "REACTIONS" (in general, less than 1 page) are completely free. However, if too many "reactions" accumulate, participants and visitors are kindly requested to wait a little for a quieter period. Alternatively, such "reactions" can be sent to the moderators, when authors are willing that these appear into a collective "batch" together with other comments on the related topic.

Following topics will be covered:

1.  Physical Perspectives on Information
     (eg, classical, thermodynamical, quantum mechanics, quantum gravity; the basic idea of
     states and of dynamical laws; information and the nature of time)

     moderation: Koichiro Matsuno kmatsuno@voscc.nagaokaut.ac.jp

2.  The Informational Dynamics of the Cell
     (intracellular and intercellular info exchanges, the cellular use of Chaos, the
     molecular-biological "production of meaning"...)

     moderation: Ray Paton R.C.Paton@csc.liv.ac.uk

3.  Information and the Nature of Consciousness
     (The consciousness issue as an important,fundamental, and necessary info topic; a survey of
     current views and difficulties from the multidisciplinary prism of information...)

     moderation: Michael Conrad conrad@cs.wayne.edu
                      Kevin Kirby  kirby@nku.edu

4.  The Information Revolution and the Problems of our Time
     (Overall nature of social info; the current info revolution and its repercussions upon the
     economic, ecological and political spheres -- the "global brain" as a new tool for survival or as
     a recipe for faster global chaos?)

     moderation: Pedro C. Marijuán marijuan@posta.unizar.es

HOW TO JOIN

To join the FIS list simply send the following command to: listserver@listas.unizar.es
SUBSCRIBE FIS Your_name
After which you will receive all postings to the FIS list.

To post a "Presentation” or "Reaction” (see above) to the FIS list send it to: fis@listas.unizar.es

To cancel your membership send the command: UNSUBSCRIBE FIS to: listserver@listas.unizar.es
Every subscribe/unsubscribe command to the listserver is confirmed through a corresponding reply.
It is important that you send all your posting to the FIS list from the email account you subscribed from.
