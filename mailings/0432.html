 Petition
 
Call to solidarity.
 
The isue is, to send a letter to the Nigerian officials on behalf of Safya
Husseini Tungar-Tudu, a young NIgerian girl, who has been convicted in her
country to the death penalty, because she became pregnant without having a
husband.

Such an act is there regarded as a severe infringement of islamic
fundamentalistic law, that in her country takes the place of criminal law.

If not heavy international pressure is exerted, within about a month Safya
will be placed in a grave, then buried up to her waist, and finally stoned
to death by the inhabitants of her village.

At this moment she is locked up in her house, where she feeds her child,
that has become a synonym to the death penalty.

She will be allowed to breast-feed during some weeks to come, then she will
be sent into the grave and killed (this has been confirmed by Amnesty
International).

 We can do something, when we write to the Nigerian embassy in Rome, to beg
the president of the Nigerian Republic for her reprieve.

However, it have to be many letters. I therefore ask you to lead this call
further to your friends (do not forget those without an e-mail-connection)
and to write at once to the embassy. When you do not act quickly, we risk
to intervene too late.
 
As a last detail: as always in such matters: the father of the child has
been acquitted because of lack of evidence. We cannot remain at the edge of
the grave and just look at this newest instance of intolerance, ignorance,
and lie. We must make a collective gesture of solidarity, by sending the
 following letter to:

 Ambasciata della Nigeria
 A l'attention de l'Ambassadeur

 00193 Roma

 or the following e-mail to: embassy@nigerian.it

 Monsieur l'Ambassadeur,

 Je vous prie de demander au President de la Republique du Nigeria de
 sauver la vie de Safya Husseini-Tungar-Tudu
