Dear Colleagues,

The concept of deixis (and deictic shift) has a strong history in
linguistics and
philosophy (e.g. Frege). I would be interested to hear of any work being
done in information
theory which parallels those developments - particulary the notion of
'referral' and
how the 'Here Now and I' experience in natural language translates into
informational situations (like the policeman on point duty...)

Deictically -

My name is John Holgate and I am an information scientist/
practitioner with a background in linguistics, philosophy and philology.

I have just completed a Masters degree in Information at the
University of Technology Sydney and hold degrees in French
and German literature and library/information science.

My specialty was knowledge management and I'm particularly
interested in 'umbrella' theories of information which could
place KM and the information professions on a scientific footing
(which IMO they sadly lack).

I am preparing a doctoral thesis on Information Language and Knowing
which explores the multifarious conceptual interactions
between the three phenomena.

I am currently Director of Library Services at
a teaching hospital in Sydney, Australia (St. George's Hospital).
I have also been a consultant on knowledge management,
portals and electronic publishing in the health industry and
have presented at various health informatics conferences.

I was delighted to discover the FIS list recently and have
been an admiring kibitzer - I look forward to all the
exciting action of the May conference.

Venceremos
