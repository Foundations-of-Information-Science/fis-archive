Who was Michael Conrad

Michael Conrad

Dr. Conrad received his A.B. from Harvard University in 1963 and Ph.D. from Stanford University in 1969. He was a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Center for Theoretical Studies, University of Miami (1969-71) and a Postdoctoral Scholar in the Department of Mathematics, University of California at Berkeley (9172-72). He was an Assistant Professor, Institute for Information Science, University of Tuebingen, West Germany (1972-75), an Associate Professor, Department of Biology, City College, City University of New York (1974-75), and an Associate Professor, Department of Computer and Communication Sciences, University of Michigan (1975-79).

Dr. Conrad has been a Visiting Scholar at Cavendish Laboratory, Cambridge University (1979 and 1982), a U.S. National Academy of Science Exchange Scientist to the former Soviet Union, and a U.S. National Science Foundation Exchange Scientist at the Molecular Biophysics Unit of the Indian Institute of Science. In 1987, he was a Japanese Ministry of Education Special Visiting Professor at the Nagaoka University of Technology, and in 1991, he was appointed a permanent Visiting Professor at Southeast University in Nanjing. He serves as an editor or board member on a number of international scientific journals and was elected to the presidency of the International Society for Molecular Electronics and Biocomputing for the 1993-94 term.

At last he was head of the Biocomputing Group at Wayne State University.

His last primary Research Interests were: Biocomputing; computational modeling (evolutionary programming) and adaptability theory.
